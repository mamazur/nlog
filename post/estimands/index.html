Estimands

Nina Loginova

In clinical trials, one of the fundamental goals is to understand the effect of a treatment or intervention. To achieve this, we need to clearly define what exactly we are trying to measure. This is where the concept of estimands becomes crucial.

An estimand is essentially the specific aspect of the treatment effect that we aim to measure. It represents the precise question we want to answer about the impact of the treatment. For instance, if a new drug is being tested, the estimand might be how much the drug reduces symptoms compared to a placebo.

There are several types of estimands, each addressing different aspects of how we measure the treatment effect. One common type is the treatment effect on the entire study population, which considers how well the treatment works if everyone follows the study protocol exactly. Another type looks at the treatment effect specifically for those participants who complete the study, providing insight into the effectiveness among those who adhere to the treatment regimen. Additionally, there are estimands that consider real-world conditions, accounting for the fact that participants might not always follow the treatment instructions perfectly.
